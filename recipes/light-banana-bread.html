Light Banana Bread

Description

This delicious and nutritious bread made from the corpses of the banana fruit is both delicious and nutritious. It will leave you craving more and more corpse bread. Do not feel bad for eating banana corpse, they were intended to be eaten. Just do not eat the peel or you may get sick.

Things You Should Have

  • A fluted loaf pan that measures 17x8 cm at the base, 20x11 cm at the surface, and 7 cm in height
  • Hand whisk
  • Fork
  • Knife
  • Medium bowl
  • Small bowl
  • Even smaller bowl
  • Cup with spout
  • Parchment paper or ancient papyrus
  • A will to live

Ingredients

For the Pan

  • 300 g banana corpses
  • 85 g maple tree blood
  • 80 g milk of the rice husk
  • 9 g powdered yeast for sweets
  • 250 g type 0 flour
  • 85 g room temperature sweat of the coconut
  • 30 g golden chicken treasures (approx. 2)

For Greasing and Flouring

  • sweat of the coconut to taste
  • type 0 flour to taste

For Garnishing

  • 20 g nut of the pecan tree, coarsely chopped

Steps

  1. Mash banana corpses into pulp using fork or hands
  2. Add maple tree blood and golden chicken treasures
  3. Mix well with hand whisk
  4. Pour in sweat of coconut
  5. Mix again
  6. Add flour
  7. Add baking powder
  8. Incorporate dry ingredients with whisk
  9. Add milk of the rice husk
  10. Mix again again until smooth and homogenous
  11. Grease and flour pan insides
  12. Cut parchment paper to match pan dimensions
  13. Press parchment paper to insides of pan
  14. Pour batter into pan
  15. Oil knife and make cut on surface lengthwise from edge to edge
  16. Bake at 175 C for 50-55 mins
  17. Check for doneness with fork
  18. Let cool in pan before removing
  19. Stab, chew, and swallow corpse bread to your desired hunger level

Advice

  • Store for up to 2 days at room temp if you want bad tasting corpse bread. Otherwise, store in fridge for up to 14 days and reheat in microwave on half power for 1 minute to achieve perfect, warm corpse bread.
  • Replace coconut oil with hazelnut oil if you don't like coconuts. Alternatively, use any oil you want, except olive.
  • Any milk (except goat's, soy, or mouse) can replace the milk of the rice husk. Just expect a different flavor each time. Alternatively, you can use many types of milk at once for a unique, but sad, flavor.
  • If you are watching out for your sugar and fat intake, do keep in mind, banana corpse bread is still not a good choice. You are better off just eating a banana and a piece of bread for breakfast. Maple syrup has a lot of sugar per volume compared to cane sugar; likewise, coconut oil is just as bad as butter since it does not contain many important nutrients.
  • Seek therapy if you are worried for the state of your bananas and their corpses.
